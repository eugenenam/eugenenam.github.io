PawPal

Amber Alert for Dogs


Background

In 2014, my dog Julie ran away from home without her collar on. It was the most terrifying moment in my life. I had never experienced a lost pet situation and was panicking. I googled "Lost Pet" and was flooded by search results. In addition, I felt overwhelmed given that I wasn't sure whether to go outside and search for my dog or to immediately create and print fliers. As a result, I decided to design a solution that digitized the process of creating lost pet fliers and notifying local shelters/hospitals.

Target Audience

Dog Owners

Goal

A mobile app that allows dog owners to create an amber alert for lost dogs. The user will be able to create a digital lost dog alert with his/her dog’s information and notify nearby users, shelters, and animal hospitals simultaneously.

Rationale

By notifying nearby users, the app widens the search network instantly and increases her chances of locating her lost pet. In addition, simplifying the features and functions caters to the needs (speed / ease of use) and emotional state (worried / overwhelmed) of a user.

User Methodology


5 User Interviews (45 - 60 mins each)

    Dog owners with varying experiences: First Time and Experienced Owners

    Age: Early 20s to Late 40s

    Device usage: Smartphone/Tablet owners


User Research Findings


Major Pain Points

    Dog care tips (food, training, exercise)

    Managing a dog’s medical information (including vet appointments)

    Missing Pet Situation
